﻿I am a first year Mechatronics Engineering student at University of Waterloo. I have always been passionate about science and technology and loved to apply scientific principle to solve various problems. Completing my schooling in India, I came to the University of Waterloo for studying engineering to follow my passion. Having an analytical mindset, I love problem solving. I am interested in Robotics and Software. 

Following my interest in engineering as well as my studies at University of Waterloo,  I have learnt variety of different programming languages like C++, C#, Java, MATLAB and LabVIEW  for software development as well as general engineering applications. I am skilled in Computer Aided Designing and have experience using AutoCAD and SolidWorks. Through my work with different student teams on campus as well as my work terms, I have developed proficiency in machining and fabrication.

Along with my studies, I am currently working as a Research Assistant with Dr Amir Khajepour in Waterloo Centre for Automotive Research (WATCAR). As a research assistant I am mainly involved in the Pneumatic Suspension Project and also assist other research projects being carried out under supervision of Dr Khajepour. My experience working in Waterloo Centre for Automotive Research has provided me many valuable skills in the field of programming for practical applications, working with real time systems, integrating ideas to solve problems, analyzing the results from research experiments, exceptional hands on experience in machining and fabrication and many more.

I am fortunate to be given the opportunity to be a Tutor with Tutoring Beyond Borders (TBB) which is a charitable organization. My responsibility as a tutor includes teaching Mathematics to a grade 10 student. I feel very delighted to work with TBB as it gives me an opportunity to help someone. I also learn a lot of new things from the students and teaching them helps me brush upon my concepts.﻿
